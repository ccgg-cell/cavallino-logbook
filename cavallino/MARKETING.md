# Cavallino Logbook — Launch Kit

## One-line pitch
A private service and provenance ledger for exotic car owners — track every maintenance interval by model era, log every shop visit, and export a clean PDF report the day you sell.

## Elevator pitch (30 sec)
Most exotic owners track service history in a shoebox of receipts or a notes app that doesn't know their car. Cavallino Logbook preloads maintenance intervals by era (classic V8, classic V12, modern NA, modern turbo), tracks every car in a multi-vehicle garage, and turns the whole history into a clean provenance PDF in one click — the kind of documentation that actually moves price at sale or auction.

## Where to post (in order of likely traction)

1. **FerrariChat (FChat)** — largest English-language Ferrari owner forum. Post in the general or marque-specific sub-forum, framed as "I built a free tool for tracking service history by model era — feedback welcome." Tool posts framed as asking for feedback outperform tool posts framed as selling.
2. **Bring a Trailer comments** — on any Ferrari/exotic listing with messy or absent service history, a tasteful one-line mention ("built a free logbook for this kind of thing if it's useful") reaches exactly the buyers who care about documentation.
3. **Facebook groups** — "Ferrari Owners," "Classic Ferrari Owners Club," and similarly named regional groups. Post the landing page link with 2–3 screenshots, not just text.
4. **Reddit** — r/Ferrari, r/cars (smaller subset), r/PelicanParts-adjacent communities for other marques if you expand beyond Ferrari templates.
5. **Local FCA (Ferrari Club of America) chapter newsletters/events** — email the chapter secretary, offer it as a free member resource. Club newsletters reach exactly the right audience with built-in trust.
6. **Specialist mechanics** — independent Ferrari/exotic specialists often want their clients to track history better. Offer them a referral angle: "send your clients here, it makes your invoices easier to track too."

## Positioning notes
- Never use the Ferrari name/logo in branding, app icon, or ads — reference the audience contextually ("for prancing-horse owners," "for exotic Italian iron") rather than using protected marks.
- Lead with the provenance/PDF export angle in resale-minded communities (BaT, FCA, classifieds); lead with the maintenance-schedule angle in daily-driver/track-day communities.
- The free tier should stay genuinely useful forever — the paywall is for the *export*, not for basic tracking. This is what earns word-of-mouth in tight-knit owner communities that are allergic to feeling upsold.

## Pricing rationale
$7/month for Pro is priced low intentionally — it's a rounding error against this audience's typical maintenance bill, so the friction to upgrade is minimal once they need a report. Consider also offering a one-time $15 "single report" purchase for owners who only need it once at point of sale, as an alternative entry point for people who won't subscribe.
